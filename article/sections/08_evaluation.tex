\section{Experimental Evaluation}
\label{sec:evaluation}

The goal of this evaluation is not to establish an optimal underwater racing controller, but to demonstrate that the benchmark, the official observation contract, the referee, and the team scoring behave as specified, and to characterize the reference baseline honestly---including where it fails.

\subsection{Protocol and Metrics}

All runs use the HoloOcean adapter with the BlueROV2 agent, seed $0$, control timestep $\Delta t=0.033$\,s, official mode (so the controller receives no ground-truth state), and the unmodified official gate apertures (requirement R4). The reported metrics are: course completion (all expected gates passed in sequence and the finish reached), completed gates, official time, penalized time of \eqref{eq:penalized}, and the counts of collisions, out-of-bounds, and stuck events. Because HoloOcean execution is not bit-deterministic across runs, single-seed results should be read as representative rather than as exact expectations; multi-seed statistics over the three tracks are deferred to future work (Section~\ref{sec:discussion}), and we are explicit about this limitation rather than reporting a single number as if it were a population mean.

The baseline completes all three courses---$12/12$, $17/17$, and $22/22$ gates---without collisions, out-of-bounds, or stuck events, with official times of $228.4$\,s, $315.5$\,s, and $542.4$\,s on Horseshoe Bay, Vertical Serpent, and Mixed Endurance respectively. These results establish that the official clean tasks are feasible under the official observation contract and that the referee accepts well-formed sequential crossings; they are infrastructure-level evidence, not a claim of optimality.

\subsection{Staggered Fleet and Team Scoring}

The bottom block of Table~\ref{tab:validation_results} reports the stable two-rover staggered demonstration on Horseshoe Bay. Both rovers finish $12/12$ gates; the leading rover is collision-free, while the trailing rover incurs a single gate-bar contact, charged at $5$\,s, so its penalized time exceeds its official time by $5$\,s. The team summary aggregates to $24/24$ gates, an elapsed time of $339.7$\,s from first release to last finish, and a penalized team time of $344.7$\,s. No inter-vehicle proximity events are recorded, which is expected: the $90$\,s start gap deliberately separates the rovers in time so that the run exercises multi-agent spawning, staggered release, independent per-rover referee state, ranking, and team aggregation \emph{without} physical interaction, until rover--rover arbitration is calibrated. We therefore present this as a valid low-contact smoke demonstration, not as evidence that close-proximity fleet racing is solved.

\subsection{Behavior Under Currents}

The clean-track performance does not transfer to disturbance conditions. In experimental current-enabled runs the rule baseline, which uses no current compensation, fails to complete Horseshoe Bay under the medium current profile in our trials (zero gates completed), and current-compensation modes are not yet able to recover clean-track performance. We report this negative result deliberately: it quantifies the gap that the current-compensation track family and future controllers must close, and it follows the benchmark principle that failures are reported honestly rather than hidden by enlarging gates, relaxing referee margins, or weakening the current profile (requirement R4).

\subsection{Reproducibility}

Every run emits a structured event log and a summary, and the repository provides the exact commands used here~\cite{holodronecompetition2026}. The single-entry configuration-driven launch and the per-track JSON files make the official runs reproducible from a single command; the fleet demonstration and the unit-test suite are likewise scripted, so the results in Table~\ref{tab:validation_results} can be regenerated end to end.
