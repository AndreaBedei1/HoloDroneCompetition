\section{Future Work}
\label{sec:discussion}

We see four concrete next steps.

\emph{(i) Cooperative fleet strategies beyond leader-follower convoying.} The leader-follower coordination of Section~\ref{sec:coordination} has already been evaluated on the deterministic kinematic substrate. It has also undergone HoloOcean diagnostic validation on clean Horseshoe Bay (Section~\ref{sec:eval_holoocean_coord}). The immediate next step is to extend that diagnostic validation to other tracks, currents, obstacles and larger fleets. Later work can examine formation keeping, cooperative overtaking, dynamic role assignment and robustness to uncertain relative localization or heavier acoustic loss. Such strategies must respect the low bandwidth and high latency of acoustic communication~\cite{stojanovic2009underwater} described in the AUV-formation literature~\cite{yang2021survey,yan2023formation}.

\emph{(ii) Competitive multi-team and heterogeneous-rover evaluation.} Extend the cooperative fleet setting to multiple rover teams or rover families with different dynamics and sensing suites. They would compete under the same referee and observation contract. This extension requires team-isolated scoring, ranking across teams, controlled interaction policies and normalization rules that compare heterogeneous platforms without hiding their physical differences.

\emph{(iii) Current-compensation track family.} Promote the analytic current field of \eqref{eq:current_sum} to \eqref{eq:vortex} into a graded official track family based on the provided \texttt{medium} and \texttt{strong} profiles. Controllers can then be developed to reject these currents. One legal direction is a cascaded design with an inner loop that servos DVL-measured body velocity to the guidance setpoint. This loop would reject the current as a disturbance without reading the true current vector, beneath outer beacon and vision guidance.

\emph{(iv) Learning-based controllers.} The official observation contract can serve directly as a reinforcement-learning interface. The state contains beacon range, bearing and elevation; depth; IMU; DVL and velocity; and camera data. The normalized command of \eqref{eq:command} is the action. A reward can combine sequential gate progress with the time and collision penalties of \eqref{eq:penalized}. A model-free policy-gradient or actor-critic method could train at scale on the deterministic kinematic adapter and then be fine-tuned in HoloOcean. This approach follows work on champion-level drone racing against a simulated opponent~\cite{kaufmann2023swift} and GPU-parallel robot-learning environments~\cite{makoviychuk2021isaacgym,brockman2016openaigym} without weakening the official observation contract.
