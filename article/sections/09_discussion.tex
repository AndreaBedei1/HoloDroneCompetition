\section{Future Work}
\label{sec:discussion}

We see six concrete next steps.

\emph{(i) Inter-vehicle collision calibration.} Sweep two BlueROV2 agents over a grid of relative longitudinal, lateral, vertical and yaw offsets, record the engine contact sensor against the geometric separation and fit the thresholds $(r_{xy},r_z,r_{\mathrm{rel}})$ of \eqref{eq:ivc} to the measured contact envelope, replacing the current conservative defaults.

\emph{(ii) Penalized fleet mode.} With calibrated thresholds, validate the \cmd{penalize} mode under controlled close-proximity scenarios in which rovers are deliberately brought within contact range, confirming that team penalties are charged once per pair event and that scoring remains stable.

\emph{(iii) Cooperative fleet strategies.} Building on the optional inter-rover acoustic channel now provided (Section~\ref{sec:fleet}), move beyond staggered, non-interacting starts to deliberate coordination, formation keeping and leader-follower passing of gate sequences, under the realistic constraints of low-bandwidth, high-latency acoustic communication~\cite{stojanovic2009underwater} and uncertain relative localization, as analyzed in the AUV-formation literature~\cite{yang2021survey,yan2023formation}.

\emph{(iv) Competitive multi-team and heterogeneous-rover evaluation.} Extend the current cooperative fleet setting to scenarios in which multiple rover teams, or different rover families with distinct dynamics and sensing suites, compete under the same referee and observation contract. This would require team-isolated scoring, ranking across teams, controlled interaction policies and normalization rules that make heterogeneous platforms comparable without hiding their physical differences.

\emph{(v) Current-compensation track family.} Promote the analytic current field of \eqref{eq:current_sum} to \eqref{eq:vortex} into a graded benchmark family (medium and strong profiles) and develop controllers that reject it. A promising legal direction is a cascaded design whose inner loop servos the DVL-measured body velocity to the guidance setpoint, rejecting the current as a disturbance without ever reading the true current vector, beneath the outer beacon and vision gate guidance.

\emph{(vi) Learning-based controllers.} The official observation contract is directly usable as a reinforcement-learning interface: the observation (beacon range, bearing and elevation; depth; IMU; DVL and velocity; and camera) is the state, the normalized command of \eqref{eq:command} is the action and a natural reward combines sequential gate progress with time and collision penalties mirroring \eqref{eq:penalized}. A model-free policy-gradient or actor-critic method could be trained at scale against the kinematic fallback adapter and fine-tuned in HoloOcean, in the spirit of champion-level drone racing trained against a simulated opponent~\cite{kaufmann2023swift} and of GPU-parallel robot-learning environments~\cite{makoviychuk2021isaacgym,brockman2016openaigym}, all without weakening the official observation contract.
