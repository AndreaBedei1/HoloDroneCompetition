\section{Discussion, Limitations and Future Work}
\label{sec:discussion}

The experiments show that completion depends on both course structure and environmental conditions. The two reference controllers clear the short clean circuit but separate on the longer track and under current; success on clean tracks does not imply robustness to disturbances. The coordination study shows that a fully distributed leader-follower layer, using only legal onboard estimates and an acoustic-inspired channel, removes the physical interaction experienced by an uncoordinated heterogeneous convoy, with the one-gate margin achieving this result at the lowest time cost. Throughout, controller-local progression and independent referee scoring remain separate. Their occasional disagreement is logged rather than reconciled, allowing the same contract to score arbitrary controllers.

\subsection{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

The evaluation has several limitations. The reference controllers are rule-based, all results are obtained in HoloOcean, and no real-vehicle or learning-based validation is included. The acoustic channel is evaluated only within the coordination task, without an independent analysis of packet loss, range or latency. The strong-current profile mainly represents a saturation condition, while the revised beacon-noise model preserves the numerical behavior of the original implementation rather than introducing newly calibrated sensor statistics. These limitations define the present scope and indicate natural directions for extending the released benchmark.

\subsection{Future Work}

Four directions follow.

\emph{(i) Cooperative fleet strategies beyond leader-follower convoying.} The leader-follower coordination of Section~\ref{sec:coordination} is validated on clean Horseshoe Bay (Section~\ref{sec:eval_holoocean_coord}). Further studies can cover other tracks, currents, obstacles and larger fleets, as well as formation keeping, cooperative overtaking, dynamic role assignment and robustness to uncertain relative localization or heavier acoustic loss. Such strategies must respect the low bandwidth and high latency of acoustic communication~\cite{stojanovic2009underwater} described in the AUV-formation literature~\cite{yang2021survey,yan2023formation}.

\emph{(ii) Competitive multi-team and heterogeneous-vehicle evaluation.} The cooperative fleet setting can be extended to multiple teams or vehicle families with different dynamics and sensing suites competing under the same referee and observation contract. This requires team-isolated scoring, ranking across teams, controlled interaction policies and normalization rules that compare heterogeneous platforms without hiding their physical differences.

\emph{(iii) Current compensation and broader disturbance validation.} The three official circuits already expose the graded \texttt{medium} and \texttt{strong} profiles of \eqref{eq:current_sum} to \eqref{eq:vortex}; the present quantitative sweep is limited to Horseshoe Bay. Controllers can be evaluated on the remaining circuits while explicitly rejecting these disturbances. One legal direction is a cascaded design with an inner loop that servos DVL-measured body velocity to the guidance setpoint, beneath outer beacon and vision guidance, without access to environmental-current telemetry.

\emph{(iv) Learning-based controllers.} The controller-visible observation and normalized action contract provide the basis for a future Gymnasium-compatible learning interface. Such an interface requires a fixed observation encoding, an explicit training reward, step-wise environment control, and validation of learned policies. Implementing and evaluating imitation- and reinforcement-learning controllers is left for future work.
