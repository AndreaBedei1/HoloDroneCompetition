\section{Vehicle, Simulator and Sensing}
\label{sec:configuration}

This section describes the simulation backend, the vehicle and its sensors, the acoustic and current models, the declarative track configuration and the official observation contract that the referee enforces.

\subsection{Simulator and Vehicle}

The benchmark architecture is not tied to a particular vehicle: adapters provide simulator state, filtered sensing and action mapping to the common race and referee layers. The reference adapter, and every HoloOcean experiment reported here, uses HoloOcean~\cite{potokar2022holoocean} with a BlueROV2-class agent~\cite{vonbenzon2022bluerov2} in direct eight-thruster mode (HoloOcean \texttt{control\_scheme}~$0$). The simulator advances at $30$ physics ticks per second. The benchmark uses a control timestep of $\Delta t=0.033$\,s, so each control step corresponds to one physics tick and the effective control rate is $\approx\!30$\,Hz.

In the reference HoloOcean adapter, a high-level command $u=[u^{\mathrm{surge}},u^{\mathrm{sway}},u^{\mathrm{heave}},u^{\mathrm{yaw}}]^{\!\top}$ is mapped to the eight BlueROV2 thrusters by a fixed mixing matrix. The four vertical thrusters receive $u^{\mathrm{heave}}$; the four horizontal thrusters receive
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:thruster_mix}
\tau_{1\text{--}4}= s_\tau\!\begin{bmatrix} u^{\mathrm{surge}}+u^{\mathrm{sway}}+\kappa\,u^{\mathrm{yaw}} \\[1pt] u^{\mathrm{surge}}-u^{\mathrm{sway}}-\kappa\,u^{\mathrm{yaw}} \\[1pt] u^{\mathrm{surge}}+u^{\mathrm{sway}}-\kappa\,u^{\mathrm{yaw}} \\[1pt] u^{\mathrm{surge}}-u^{\mathrm{sway}}+\kappa\,u^{\mathrm{yaw}} \end{bmatrix},
\end{equation}
with yaw-mixing gain $\kappa=0.35$ and thruster scale $s_\tau$, each component clamped to the thruster limit. A controller may instead return the eight thruster values directly; the high-level interface is the default because it is simpler and vehicle-agnostic (a controller expresses intended body-frame motion while the framework handles the mixing and depth-safety), whereas direct thruster control is available when finer actuator authority is wanted.

\subsection{Acoustic Beacon Model}

Let $(\hat{n},\hat{r}_g,\hat{s}_g)$ be the right-handed gate frame of \eqref{eq:gate_frame}, with $\hat{n}$ the passage normal and $\hat{s}_g$ the gate-up axis. Each gate carries one beacon, placed at $b_g=c_g+\delta_n \hat{n}+\delta_r \hat{r}_g+\delta_s \hat{s}_g$ relative to the gate center $c_g$ (default offset $(0,0,0.35)$\,m along $\hat{s}_g$). For a receiver at position $p_r$ with heading $\psi$, let $\boldsymbol{\delta}=b_g-p_r$ and $\rho=\lVert\boldsymbol{\delta}\rVert$. The beacon reports range, a yaw-relative bearing, an elevation and a signal strength:
\begin{align}
\label{eq:beacon}
\rho &= \lVert\boldsymbol{\delta}\rVert,\\
\beta &= \operatorname{wrap}\!\big(\operatorname{atan2}(\delta_y,\delta_x)-\psi\big),\\
\varepsilon &= \operatorname{atan2}\!\big(\delta_z,\,\textstyle\sqrt{\delta_x^2+\delta_y^2}\big),\\
\mathrm{SS} &= \max\!\Big(0,\,1-\tfrac{\rho}{\rho_{\max}}\Big),\nonumber
\end{align}
where $\rho_{\max}$ is the beacon range and $\operatorname{wrap}(\cdot)$ maps to $(-180^\circ,180^\circ]$. A reading is invalid beyond $\rho_{\max}$ or when a Bernoulli dropout fires. In the noisy acoustic mode, independent Gaussian noise $\mathcal{N}(0,\sigma_b)$ is added to $\beta$, $\varepsilon$ and $\rho$; the official tracks use $\sigma_b\in\{0.2,0.6\}$ and dropout probabilities up to $0.04$. Only the beacon of the current target gate is active in the default \emph{active-when-target} mode, so the beacon provides a target cue but not a map of the whole course.

\subsection{Current Field Model}

The current field is a sum of analytic primitives evaluated at the vehicle position $p$ and time $t$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:current_sum}
\mathbf{v}_c(p,t)=\sum_{j} \mathbf{v}_j(p,t),
\end{equation}
where each primitive is one of the following. A \emph{constant} term contributes a fixed velocity $\mathbf{v}_0$. A \emph{localized jet} centered at $c_j$ with radius $R_j$ contributes, for $\lVert p-c_j\rVert\le R_j$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:jet}
\mathbf{v}_j=\mathbf{v}_0\, \omega(d), \qquad
\omega(d)=\begin{cases} \max\!\big(0,1-\tfrac{d}{R_j}\big) & \text{linear,}\\[2pt] \exp\!\big(-\tfrac{d^2}{2\sigma^2}\big) & \text{Gaussian,} \end{cases}
\end{equation}
with $d=\lVert p-c_j\rVert$ and $\sigma=\max(R_j/2,10^{-6})$ and zero outside $R_j$. A \emph{sinusoidal} term modulates one axis, $v_{j,\mathrm{axis}}(t)=v_{0,\mathrm{axis}}+A\sin(2\pi f t+\phi)$. A \emph{vortex} of radius $R_j$, tangential speed $V_t$ and vertical speed $V_z$ contributes, with $r=\sqrt{\Delta x^2+\Delta y^2}$ and tangent $\hat{\theta}=(-\Delta y,\Delta x)/r$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:vortex}
\mathbf{v}_j=\big(\hat{\theta}_x\,V_t\,\omega(r),\;\hat{\theta}_y\,V_t\,\omega(r),\;V_z\,\omega(r)\big).
\end{equation}
The provided \texttt{strong} profile superposes a constant set-flow of $[0.75,1.05,0]$\,m/s, two Gaussian jets, a vortex and a vertical sinusoid; the \texttt{medium} profile scales these magnitudes by $0.5$. The current physically perturbs the vehicle when the HoloOcean build exposes ocean-current coupling; otherwise the field is exposed as telemetry only.

\subsection{Declarative Track Configuration}

A track is a self-contained JSON file. Its principal sections are \cmd{race} (name, benchmark task, timing mode, laps and duration), \cmd{world} (bounds and environment), \cmd{start} and \cmd{finish}, \cmd{track} and \cmd{gates} (the ordered gate sequence with centers, passage directions and aperture sizes), \cmd{beacon}, \cmd{currents}, \cmd{obstacles}, \cmd{participants} (vehicle, spawn, sensors, controller and optional \cmd{start\_delay\_s}) and \cmd{referee} (gate validation, penalties and scoring). A named \cmd{benchmark\_task} (\cmd{clean\_gate}, \cmd{obstacle\_gate}, \cmd{current\_gate} or \cmd{multi\_rov}) selects a preset overlay. Listing~\ref{lst:trackjson} shows a representative excerpt.

\begin{lstlisting}[caption={Representative excerpt of a track configuration.}, label={lst:trackjson}]

\subsection{Official Observation Contract}
\label{sec:obs_contract}

The controller \texttt{step} function receives a dictionary with the fields in Table~\ref{tab:observation}. In official mode the runner removes pose, location, rotation, dynamics, exact-position, debug ground-truth and exact-gate-geometry fields, then keeps only the configured allow-list of sensor names; numeric arrays are made JSON-safe while image arrays are preserved. Controllers do not receive the configured environment current vector. The runner removes it from official observations even if an allow-list entry requests it; the vector is available only as diagnostic telemetry in non-official runs. A controller must therefore infer current effects from its onboard sensing (e.g.\ the DVL or velocity-sensor residual), not read the current directly. The intended information contract is acoustic target guidance, onboard sensing and race progress, not global state. When the optional inter-rover acoustic channel is enabled (Section~\ref{sec:fleet}), the observation additionally carries a \cmd{comms} inbox whose messages are authored by other controllers and therefore contain only information those controllers may legally observe.
