\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper presented \mra{}, a configurable benchmark for autonomous underwater gate racing in HoloOcean. A replaceable adapter separates vehicle and simulator integration from the race model, while an official observation contract keeps controller inputs distinct from the privileged state used by the independent referee. The same evaluation principles support individual vehicles and cooperative fleets with reproducible configuration, scoring and logging.

The experiments show that performance depends on course structure and environmental conditions and that success on clean tracks does not imply robustness to currents. The fleet studies further show that local leader-follower coordination can reduce physical interaction, although its behavior depends on the selected policy and local progress estimates. Differences between controller-local progression and referee evaluation reinforce the need to keep navigation and scoring independent. Overall, \mra{} provides a practical foundation for comparing underwater autonomy methods and studying perception-based control, disturbance rejection and multi-vehicle coordination.
