\begin{abstract}
Autonomous racing turns perception, planning, and control into a single measurable task under reproducible time pressure, and ground and aerial racing platforms have become effective evaluation substrates for this reason. Underwater robotics, however, lacks a comparable gate-racing benchmark: existing marine simulators provide vehicles and sensors but not a compact racing protocol that couples damped vehicle dynamics, range-limited and low-bandwidth sensing, currents, obstacles, an independent referee, and fleet coordination. This work presents \mra{}, a configurable evaluation layer built around the HoloOcean simulator and a BlueROV2-class underwater vehicle. The central design principle is a strict separation between the autonomy stack and the evaluation: a controller observes only a documented official observation, while an independent referee uses privileged state to validate gate crossings, collisions, out-of-bounds and stuck conditions, timeouts, ranking, and team scoring. The benchmark is declarative---tracks, sensors, currents, obstacles, participants, and scoring are specified in a single configuration---and a custom controller is integrated by implementing three methods. We formalize the benchmark model, the gate-crossing and scoring rules, and the staggered fleet/team aggregation, and we report HoloOcean validation in which a deterministic rule-based baseline completes three official clean tracks. In clean, current-free conditions the baseline is collision-free; physical gate contact emerges under disturbance currents, which we report honestly rather than suppress. A stable two-rover staggered fleet run completes Horseshoe Bay with independent per-rover summaries and a single aggregated team score. The goal of \mra{} is not to provide an optimal underwater racing controller, but a reproducible benchmark and evaluation contract on which such controllers---including learning-based ones---can be built and compared; current compensation, close-proximity fleet racing, and empirically calibrated rover--rover penalties are scoped explicitly as open extensions.
\end{abstract}
