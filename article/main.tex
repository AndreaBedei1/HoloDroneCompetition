\title{\mra: A Configurable HoloOcean Benchmark for Underwater Gate Racing and Team-Level Fleet Evaluation}

\author{\IEEEauthorblockN{Andrea Bedei, Lorenzo Bacchiani, Giovanni Pau and Roberto Girau}
\IEEEauthorblockA{Department of Computer Science and Engineering, University of Bologna, Italy}
}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We present \textbf{Marine Race Arena}, a configurable and reproducible framework for developing and evaluating autonomous systems in underwater gate-racing tasks. Complete race scenarios can be defined through declarative configurations, including tracks, vehicles, sensors, currents, obstacles, rules, penalties and starting conditions. The benchmark strictly separates autonomy from evaluation: controllers operate exclusively from onboard sensors, simulated acoustic beacons and optional inter-vehicle communication, while an independent referee uses privileged simulator state only to validate gate crossings, enforce the rules and compute the final results. The framework supports both single-vehicle trials and cooperative multi-vehicle scenarios, while providing a simple interface for integrating custom controllers. The release includes three ready-to-use tracks, two camera- and beacon-assisted reference controllers, leader-follower coordination tools, structured logging and automated validation procedures. Experiments conducted in HoloOcean demonstrate the ability of the benchmark to consistently evaluate course completion, timing, collisions, robustness to currents and fleet coordination. Marine Race Arena therefore provides a common platform for comparing underwater perception, navigation, control and cooperation strategies under repeatable and progressively more challenging conditions.
\end{abstract}
